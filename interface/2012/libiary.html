Shyh-Mei Ho IMS Transaction and DB2: Perfect Match Your IT Enterprise is an evolving work of art. New technologies, standards, and capabilities mean that you have many choices in how you architect that evolution. At the center of your IT is IBM Mainframe with IMS transaction manager and DB2, which consistently deliver new methods of enterprise integration, enabling you to retain your superlative elements of speed, stability, and security. Juliet Candee MF Banking Customers Active-Active Solution Banking customers in GCG today already have continuous availability/disaster recovery solution for their core business services. These solutions allow them to recover their business in a few hours in the event of a site failure or a disaster. Today, business requirements have placed more challenges on them to reduce planned and unplanned system and application outages to a few minutes. To achieve this goal, many of the bank customers will consider adopting a 2-city 3-center active-active solution - intra-city active-active sites and a remote city disaster recovery site. This presentation will describe how GDPS/Active-Active Sites is used in GCG banking customers' 2C3C continuous availability/disaster recovery architecture.
Serge Bourbonnais DB2 Replication for Business Continuity Many businesses must operate 24/7 and outages are unacceptably costly. Downtime is caused by failures, bugs, human errors, system overloads, disasters, but more often by planned upgrades, migrations, and maintenance. Q Rep is an asynchronous software replication technology based on log capture/transaction replay, which can replicate large volumes of transactions across unlimited distance, often sub-second. Sites can be of different versions and capacity. Q Replication extends DB2 HA features to provide multi-site continuous availability and improved application response times of business critical applications. Q Replication is a strategic component of the GDPS Active/Active solution offering. Deployment of Q Replication needs to consider workload and database characteristics, such as non-logged operations, large batch jobs, identity columns, triggers, schema evolution, operations, performance and recovery objectives.
Alvin Yeung Workload Modelling on Mainframe Mainframe CPU power normally measured in MIPS is expensive, yet critical to ensure sustained processing of trades that may vary due to various business drivers. We look into various mathematical models to predict likely system utilization in order to proactively manage MIPS allocation. A tool is being developed to visualize the models, with capability to fine tune model's steadiness degree, adapt new data to refine models and use industry KPIs to illustrate model fitness.
